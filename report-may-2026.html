This Week's Notes

This was the most active restructuring week since we took over the account. Three major themes ran in parallel. First, account hygiene: a full negative keyword audit identified conflicting broad negatives blocking seven active ad groups, the General Negatives shared list was found missing from all three fuel campaigns and applied, and 68 ad groups generating zero conversions were paused to concentrate budget on what is working. Second, the first hardware campaign launched on May 5: KF Hardware – Composite Decking is live with four themed ad groups, 18 phrase match keywords, and 15 sitelinks. Early CTRs are strong. Third, the account was repositioned for the off-season: heating oil and coal groups paused, budget shifted from B&G to Gas Bottle (BBQ season starting) and the new hardware campaign.

Brand and General Search recorded 0 conversions all week despite 834 clicks. A full investigation confirmed this is a tracking problem, not a campaign problem. Two GA4 measurement IDs are firing on every page of the website, and the property linked to Google Ads is only capturing 11% of sessions. The remaining 89% of Google Ads clicks go untracked — meaning purchases happen but Google Ads cannot see them. A consolidated fix request was sent to the client on May 5 for forwarding to Graphedia. This is the single most important outstanding item: without the fix, the algorithm has no signal to optimise on when the heating oil season resumes in September.

Changes Made
Optimisations This Week

Google Ads and GA4 account actions taken May 1–8, 2026.

🔧

Conflicting Negatives Removed and General Negatives Applied (May 4)

A full negative keyword audit across Brand and General Search identified several broad match negatives that were blocking active ad groups from serving. Three conflicting negatives were removed: "price" BROAD (blocking Heating Oil Prices Near Me Today, Best Home Heating Oil Prices Wexford, and Oil Prices Wexford ad groups), "prices" BROAD (blocking the same groups), and "cheapest" BROAD (blocking Cheapest Oil Prices Wexford). The competitor negative "top oil" BROAD was changed to EXACT so the competitor brand remains blocked while the Top Heating Oil Supplier Wicklow ad group is unblocked.

Separately, the General Negatives shared list was found missing from all three KF Fuel campaigns. This list blocks non-commercial queries such as photos, discount code searches, YouTube searches, and job-seeker terms. It has now been applied to Brand and General Search, Home Heating Oil Search, and Gas Bottle and Cylinders. The firewood BROAD negative in the Fuel Campaigns - Hardware Exclusions list was also removed — Kenny Fuels sells firewood, so it was incorrectly blocking all firewood ad groups.

🔧

Ad Group-Level Negatives Added: Brand Ad Group and Fuel Oil For Home Heating (May 4–5)

Search term audits identified two categories of irrelevant traffic passing through into active ad groups after the General Negatives list was applied.

Kenny Fuels brand ad group (9 phrase negatives added): kevin building supplies, a142 mesh, discount code, petrol station, service station, building materials, decorative stone, landscaping, gravel. These were non-fuel, non-brand searches pulling through because the General Negatives list had not been applied and the brand ad group was matching broadly.

Fuel Oil For Home Heating (10 competitor phrase negatives added): o neills fuels, burkes oil, klass oil, capitol oil, corrib oil, emo oil, certas, texoil, atmos boilers, bunded tank. Competitor fuel brand searches appearing in search terms — clicks on these will not convert for Kenny Fuels.

Campaign-level (May 5, 6 negatives added): kevin, zf, 20w50, boilers (broad match); kiln dried shavings, wicklow wood fuels (phrase match). Added following a May 1–4 search term review on Brand and General Search.

✅

GA4 Conversion Tracking Cleaned Up (May 5 and May 8)

A full audit of GA4 key events identified four incorrectly configured events that were inflating conversion counts throughout April. These have now been removed or corrected.

Removed on May 5: view_item (product page views were counting as conversions — removed as key event), Purchase_2 (broken duplicate tag, firing 84 times with €0 revenue), ads_conversion_Checkout_1 (duplicate of begin_checkout, 229 fires with €0 revenue), ads_conversion_Shopping_Cart_1 (duplicate of add_to_cart, 32 fires with €0 revenue).

Removed on May 8: begin_checkout and add_to_cart unmarked as GA4 key events. Only purchase remains as a key event. Begin_checkout and add_to_cart are still recorded as GA4 events — they just no longer count as conversions imported into Google Ads. This gives a cleaner picture of actual purchase intent.

Google Ads conversion actions reviewed: 17 active actions audited. Only two are set to Primary (count toward the Conversions column): Calls from ads (phone calls directly from Google Ads) and Kenny Fuels GA4 (web) purchase. All others are Secondary — recorded but not used for bidding.

🔧

Dual GA4 Tag Root Cause Identified (May 5)

A full investigation was conducted into why Brand and General Search shows 0 conversions in Google Ads while GA4 shows purchase revenue attributed to Google Ads sessions. The root cause has been identified.

Two GA4 measurement IDs are firing on every page of the kennyfuels.ie website: G-0RM5FH1MST (fired by the PixelYourSite plugin, capturing approximately 89% of sessions) and G-BMD8SXLKC0 (fired by a direct gtag.js script). The Google Ads account is linked to property 396621665. That property is only capturing 11% of sessions — meaning for every 100 visitors from Google Ads, only 11 are being tracked in the right property. The other 89 go to a different property and their conversions are never reported back to Google Ads.

A consolidated fix request was sent to the client on May 5 for forwarding to Graphedia. Required actions: (1) confirm which measurement ID belongs to property 396621665 and remove the duplicate tag from the website; (2) fix the Contact Us Form and Book Appointment conversion tags in GTM; (3) install Google's phone snippet for website call tracking so that calls made after clicking an ad are captured; (4) add form ID differentiation so Contact, Booking, and Quote forms are tracked separately rather than as a single form_submit event.

Brand and General Search: Bidding Strategy Updated (May 6)

The Brand and General Search campaign was switched from a portfolio Maximize Conversion Value strategy to a standard campaign-level Maximize Conversion Value strategy. The portfolio strategy was blocking the ability to set a maximum CPC bid limit on the campaign. The switch means the algorithm now optimises on this campaign's data only, rather than pooling signals across a portfolio.

A maximum CPC ceiling of €2.50 was attempted to control costs on non-brand ad groups. This is not currently available in the Google Ads UI for this campaign type, and the API returned a context error. The €50/day daily budget (as of May 7) acts as the practical ceiling. Average CPCs trended down over the week from €0.85 to €0.52 — the negative keyword fixes from May 4 are reducing wasteful high-CPC queries.
