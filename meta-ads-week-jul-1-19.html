The honest, complete picture is mixed, not unambiguously good news. Verified revenue grew 25x and the claimed-vs-verified gap narrowed sharply (42.0x → 12.1x) — both real signs of measurement quality improving. But real net loss deepened in absolute euros (−€1,475 → up to −€2,511), because spend nearly doubled (Gas Cylinders and Heating Oil launched Jun 25-26 and weren't running for most of the June comparison window) while real revenue, though up sharply in percentage terms, grew off a base of almost nothing. This directly matches the loss range in What It's Worth below, this isn't a separate, more positive story, it's the same one with the trend added.

🔧 A real bug was found and fixed this period: the script used to identify Meta-attributed orders in WooCommerce's export was only matching one of two formats Meta campaign tags appear in (space-separated vs. URL-encoded), silently dropping some real orders. Fixed - the verified figures above reflect the corrected count. Flagging this for transparency: it added €220 to this period's verified revenue, it doesn't change the overall picture above.
