Audit Summary

The 3-month audit covers March through May 2026, the final weeks of the heating season through to full off-season. Total spend of €3,944 generated €465,786 in attributed purchase revenue at a 118x overall ROAS. However, the monthly trend tells a starkly different story: March delivered 270x ROAS at a €1.10 cost per purchase, while May closed at 49x ROAS and €5.53 CPA. The account was not adjusted for seasonality, budgets remained flat at €60/day while demand collapsed, resulting in €1,852 being spent in May for 335 purchases versus €933 in March for 850 purchases.

Two structural gaps define the account at takeover: no retargeting has run in any period covered by this audit, leaving 1,745 abandoned add-to-carts completely unaddressed; and gas and heating oil, the business's primary product lines, have had zero paid advertising since late 2024. With September heating season 3 months away, building and testing those campaigns begins now. The account balance stands at €151.30, critically low and requiring immediate top-up before any June changes can take effect.

Audit Findings
What We Found

Issues identified across the 3-month audit period.

🚨

Account Balance Critical: €151.30 Remaining

At €60/day current spend, the account has less than 3 days of budget remaining. The account could go dark before any June strategy changes take effect. Balance top-up from Ciaran is the single most urgent action, everything else is blocked until this is resolved.

🚨

Budget Not Adjusted for Off-Season: Spend Doubled While Performance Halved

From March to May, spend increased 98% (€933 → €1,852) while purchases fell 61% (850 → 335) and ROAS collapsed from 270x to 49x. The previous manager kept all four campaigns at flat €15/day budgets through a seasonal transition that fundamentally changed demand. €1,852 was spent in May for off-season Firewood clicks that were never going to convert.

🚨

Zero Retargeting Running: 1,745 Abandoned ATCs Unaddressed

In 3 months: 1,745 add-to-carts, 1,812 checkout initiations, not a single retargeting campaign to follow up. The BOF_Retargeting campaign has been paused for the entire audit period. These are the highest-intent audiences available and they are being abandoned completely. Retargeting warm audiences typically converts at 2–5x the ROAS of cold traffic.

🚨

Gas & Heating Oil: Core Products: Have No Active Campaigns

Kenny Fuels' primary business is fuel. No gas or heating oil campaign has run in the 3-month audit period. September heating season is 3 months away, campaigns need to be built, tested, and out of the learning phase well before demand arrives. Planning starts now.

⚠️

Compost Trees Creative Receiving 7x Less Budget Than Inferior Ad

Ad 2 Compost Trees: 95.9x ROAS on €72.60 spend. Ad 1 Compost: 49.6x ROAS on €525.05 spend. Meta's algorithm is allocating the majority of budget to the lower-performing creative. This is a structural issue with running two ads in a single ad set, the algorithm settled on the wrong one. Fix required for June.

⚠️

Account Frequency at 7.14: Audiences Are Burning Out

7+ average frequency over 3 months on a reach of 145k means the same people have been shown these ads repeatedly. CTR declined from 3.41% in March to 2.40% in May, a direct symptom of creative fatigue. New creatives and expanded or refreshed audiences are needed heading into June.

⚠️

No Conversion API (CAPI): Browser Pixel Only

All tracking relies on browser-side pixel. Post-iOS14, browser tracking typically misses 15–30% of conversions due to tracking prevention and ad blockers. Given the high average order values on firewood and fuel products, each missed conversion is significant. CAPI installation is a week-one priority once pixel partner access is confirmed.
