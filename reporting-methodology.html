How to Read the Profitability Numbers

A standing reference for how blended CPA, the Meta/WooCommerce attribution gap, and real profit ranges are calculated across every Kenny Fuels report. This page doesn't change period to period the way the weekly and monthly reports do; it explains the method behind them so the numbers are auditable, not just asserted.

The Problem
Two Numbers, One Truth We Can't Fully See

Every Meta report shows two different figures for the same purchases: what Meta's pixel claims, and what WooCommerce's own order data can independently confirm. They disagree by a wide margin, and per Graphedia (confirmed Jul 7, 2026), that gap cannot be closed by any tracking fix, it's a permanent limit of what's legally trackable once a visitor declines marketing cookies.

Because that gap can't be closed, every profitability question has to be answered as a range, not a single number: a defensible floor (only what WooCommerce can verify) and a ceiling (Meta's own claim, known to be inflated). The true figure sits somewhere in between, and which end it's closer to can't currently be determined.

The Method

🔒 Neither end is "the answer" on its own. The floor is provably real but almost certainly too low, it excludes every consent-declining visitor Meta may have genuinely influenced. The ceiling is Meta's own number and known to include traffic it didn't verifiably drive. Reporting only the ceiling (the traditional approach) overstates performance; reporting only the floor understates it. Showing both is the honest answer until something narrows the range.
The Missing Piece
From Revenue to Real Profit

Everything above is revenue. Kenny Fuels runs on tight margins, so revenue isn't profit, and a strong ROAS can still be a loss once the actual cost of the product is factored in. This is the one input only Ciaran can supply: approximate gross margin % by product category.

💡 Worked example, illustrative margins only, not Kenny Fuels' real numbers: Compost generated €6,200 revenue in June on €687 spend (9.0x ROAS, looks like the best campaign in the account). At a 15% margin, gross profit is €930, minus the €687 spend leaves €243 real profit. At an 8% margin, gross profit is €496, which is less than the €687 spent, a loss, despite the 9x ROAS. The margin percentage is what decides which of those two stories is true.

Reading The Reports
What Each Label Means

Every figure in a Kenny Fuels report now carries one of these two labels. This is the key.

WOOCOMMERCE-VERIFIED

Comes from the site's own order data (WooCommerce Attribution field), independent of any ad platform's self-reporting. Trustworthy as a floor, but understates true Meta influence because consent-declining visitors can't be tracked back to Meta at all.

META SELF-REPORTED

Comes from Meta's own pixel and Ads Manager. Useful for comparing campaigns against each other (it's the only figure with per-campaign resolution), but known to overstate true attributable revenue account-wide, so it should never stand alone as "the" profit number.

In Practice
How This Changes What Gets Reported
01

Every "was it profitable" question gets a range answer

Never a single figure. Floor (verified) and ceiling (Meta's claim), both shown, both labelled.

02

Recommendations stay conditional until margin data lands

"Hold, pending margin confirmation" rather than a confident spend-up or spend-down call, on any campaign where the floor and ceiling would lead to different decisions.

03

The permanent-limitation disclaimer stays current

Language never implies the consent-tracking gap is "in progress" or "being fixed." It isn't, and won't be, per Graphedia's Jul 7 confirmation.

04

This page updates if the method changes

If margin data arrives, if the attribution gap narrows for any reason, or if a new measurement approach gets adopted, this reference gets updated in place rather than left stale while the period reports move on.
