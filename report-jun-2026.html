June 1–30. First full month under Outposter management (takeover Jun 1). Two new campaigns launched in the final week (Gas Cylinders Jun 25, Heating Oil Jun 26). Off-season demand is the primary context for lower ROAS versus historical.

First month context: off-season + two new campaigns in learning The previous agency's May Meta numbers (49x ROAS, 335 purchases, €91,304 revenue) reflected the tail of the winter heating season — a naturally high-demand period for firewood and heating products. June is mid-summer: firewood retail slows significantly, Gas Cylinders is new with no historical data, and Heating Oil launched with 0 purchases due to a page speed issue on the landing page (Graphedia fix outstanding). June's 7.0x ROAS reflects these structural realities, not a campaign performance problem. The off-season ROAS benchmark from Q2 2026 (January–May, previous management) was 79x — June shows the seasonal floor under a deliberately restructured account.
Pipeline is stronger than ROAS suggests Checkout initiations across all campaigns reached 115 in June (€38,246 in pipeline value). The Heating Oil campaign alone generated 16 checkout initiations (€9,235 estimated value) with 4 completed purchases. The primary cause of lost checkouts is landing page load speed: TTFB 2.7s on mobile (target under 0.8s), LCP 4.1s (target under 2.5s). 39% of link clicks that reach the page are bouncing before it loads. Graphedia has been briefed — once fixed, Heating Oil conversion rate should improve materially.
