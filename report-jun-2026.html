Profitability

Per your request to judge spend on profit rather than revenue or ROAS alone.

Gross profit by campaign: pending margin data Cost per conversion is already shown for every campaign in the June Summary table above, ranging from €23.34 (Brand Search) to €38.55 (Solar) — Hardware is the outlier at €35.94 per conversion against a much lower per-conversion tracked value (€90) than the fuel campaigns. I've sent this ask separately and it's still unanswered. Once I have those figures, this section will show real profit per campaign, and the recommendations below will be based on profit rather than ROAS, as requested.
Why this matters more than the headline ROAS number June's blended 11.0x ROAS (9.3x excluding Solar) is a useful signal, but it doesn't tell you whether June was profitable given Kenny Fuels' tight margins. Brand Search and HHO both generated strong tracked conversion value on modest spend and low cost per conversion — likely the two safest bets on a profit basis even before real margin data arrives. Hardware carries the highest cost per conversion (€35.94) against the lowest per-conversion value (€90) of any active campaign, which makes it the most margin-sensitive one to watch once real numbers are in: it could easily be the least profitable campaign in the account even though its ROAS (2.5x) isn't dramatically lower than the others.
