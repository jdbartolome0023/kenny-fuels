⚠️
New this period: the gap isn't really an attribution bug, it's a metric mismatch
  • Every prior report treated this gap as a WooCommerce attribution problem (the Jul 8 UTM fix not yet showing an effect). That's still partly true, but a deeper check this period found the bigger cause: Google's own "conversion value" figure is mostly built from flat per-call assumptions (~€220/call) and Kenergy Solar's synthetic €12,000/lead rule, not real transaction values at all.
  • The one Google Ads conversion action that does carry a real transaction value is the GA4 purchase import, fixed Jul 17 after an 8-week outage (see What We Did). It's recorded €54.20 total since — against €11,845.56 in real orders over just the last 3 days of this period (Jul 17-19). Escalated to Graphedia Jul 20 with that evidence.
  • What this means for the numbers above: the 82.5% "real vs. platform" figure is genuinely good news — it's up sharply from 47.8% and 26.3% — but it's not proof the UTM/attribution fix is working. It mostly reflects that this period's real order mix happened to land close to what the flat-value assumptions predict. The GA4 fix, once it actually produces real per-order data, is what would let this comparison mean what it looks like it means.
  • Confirmed noise, excluded from both figures above: 25 orders (€4,381.71) tagged "Source: Google" are Compost, Sand+Stones, or Coal — categories Google runs zero campaigns for. Standing exclusion since Jul 15/17.
  • Re-checked this period: WooCommerce still isn't tagging individual campaigns. Every Google-attributed order in this period's export, all 114 of them, still shows the same generic "Source: Google" value with no campaign-level detail. The Jul 8 UTM fix hasn't produced any visible change on the WooCommerce side yet.
