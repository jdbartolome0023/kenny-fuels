⚠️
Correction from last period's report: the Jul 8 fix has not worked, and we don't yet know why
  • The last report said this gap was a fixable configuration issue, resolved Jul 8, and expected to narrow as post-fix orders accumulated. That was the reasonable expectation at the time, but it turned out to be wrong.
  • The UTM parameters (utm_source=google&utm_medium=cpc) are confirmed live and correctly configured on the Google Ads side, verified directly via the API.
  • Zero of the 130 site orders placed since Jul 8 show any paid-search-specific attribution tag in WooCommerce. Every one still falls into the same generic "Source: Google" bucket that existed before the fix. No improvement at all.
  • Root cause not yet identified. It sits somewhere between the ad click and WooCommerce's own attribution logic. Three specific checks (WooCommerce's Order Attribution settings, whether Complianz is blocking the tracking script the same way it blocks Meta's pixel for non-consented visitors, and a couple of GTM tag coverage gaps) were sent to Graphedia Jul 13. No response yet.
  • What this means for the numbers above: the "Source: Google" bucket (74 orders, €34,254) is a mixed bucket that can include Google Business Profile clicks, Gmail links, and other non-ads Google referrals alongside genuine paid clicks, with no way to currently separate them. Its total being roughly comparable in scale to Google Ads' own reported figure is reassuring, but should not be read as a confirmed reconciliation. It could be a real match, or real paid-click revenue running under-counted while unrelated traffic pads the bucket back up. Treat this the same way Meta's numbers are treated: directionally useful, not a verified figure, until Graphedia responds.

Investigating why the campaign went 3 straight days with zero impressions uncovered a bigger issue: the 3 ad groups built in the June restructure (41 live keywords) had never had an ad added to them. The only ad that existed in the whole campaign was disapproved for a broken destination URL. Built 3 new ads on a verified-working landing page. The campaign had been effectively unable to serve for an unknown stretch before this fix.

✓

Fixed a Broken Sitelink Serving 404s on Hardware

An active sitelink on the Hardware campaign was pointing directly at a confirmed-broken category page. Replaced with the correct working URL. This also closed out a previously-flagged Graphedia billable-work item. The fix lived entirely on the Google Ads side, no development cost needed.

⚠

WooCommerce Attribution Investigation Opened

Confirmed the Jul 8 UTM fix, while correctly configured, is producing no detectable change in WooCommerce's order attribution. Three specific technical checks sent to Graphedia Jul 13 (Order Attribution settings, Complianz script categorization, GTM tag coverage). See The Real Picture above for full detail.
