Strategy Pivot · Critical Finding
What Goes on Shopping
Heating oil and diesel are not a practical fit for Google Shopping. Three factors make this the case, each on its own creating significant challenges for a viable campaign.
  • 💰
    Price accuracy. Google Shopping requires the price in the product feed to exactly match the price on the website at all times. Heating oil prices change daily or weekly. Any mismatch triggers automatic disapproval. Managing a live fuel price feed accurately enough to avoid continuous disapprovals is not practical. This requirement is documented under Google Shopping ads policies.
  • ⚠️
    Dangerous products policy. Google's policy prohibits products that "cause damage, harm, or injury", and its automated review system flags flammable materials. Fuel products are likely to trigger this review. Full policy: Google Merchant Center: Dangerous products. Note: heating oil is not explicitly named in the policy. If fuel products are submitted to a feed in future, the actual GMC disapproval reason will be the definitive confirmation of which rule applies.
  • 🛒
    Product type mismatch. Google Shopping is built for fixed-price, add-to-cart products where the customer can complete a purchase immediately. Heating oil is ordered by delivery area in variable quantities. The product structure itself is incompatible with how a Shopping feed works.
💡
The live price feed development being scoped with Graphedia is not needed for Shopping. The existing search campaign continues as normal for heating oil. No change needed there.

Why September 1?
📈
Algorithm learning takes 2–4 weeks. Google needs time to learn which searches convert before it can optimise spend. Launching Sept 1 gives the campaigns 4 weeks to learn before the October heating season peak.
🏆
Fencing and decking have a June–August peak too. Launching the feed now would also capture the tail end of summer hardware demand while the algorithm is warming up.

To unlock this, Kenny Fuels needs at least 100 verified reviews collected through Google Customer Reviews (a free Google programme). There are currently 5 Google reviews on record, all from 7–8 years ago.
