Month at a Glance
June Summary

7 campaigns. June 1–30. Gas Cylinders and Heating Oil data covers only 5–6 days (launched late June).

First month context: off-season + two new campaigns in learning The previous agency's May Meta numbers (49x ROAS, 335 purchases, €91,304 revenue) reflected the tail of the winter heating season: a naturally high-demand period for firewood and heating products. June is mid-summer: firewood retail slows significantly, Gas Cylinders is new with no history, and Heating Oil launched with 0 purchases due to a landing page speed issue (Graphedia fix outstanding). June's 7.0x ROAS reflects these structural realities, not a campaign performance problem. The off-season ROAS benchmark from the previous setup (Q1–Q2 2026 average) was in the 79x range. June shows the seasonal floor under a deliberately restructured account.
Pipeline is stronger than purchases suggest Checkout initiations across all campaigns reached 115 in June, representing approximately €38,246 in pipeline value. The Heating Oil campaign alone generated 16 checkout initiations (estimated €9,235 value) with 4 completed purchases. The primary cause of lost checkouts is landing page load speed: TTFB 2.7s on mobile (target under 0.8s), LCP 4.1s (target under 2.5s). Graphedia has been briefed: once fixed, Heating Oil conversion rate should improve materially.

Meta-Attributed Sales: Two Ways of Counting

Meta Ads reports what it thinks it drove. The site's own order data (WooCommerce) reports what it actually credits to a Meta/paid social source. The two numbers currently disagree by a wide margin.

Source Purchases Revenue % of Total Site Revenue
Meta Ads (self-reported) 75 €20,793 17.2%
WooCommerce's own attribution 4 €807 0.7%
Gap ~19x ~26x

WooCommerce attribution based on order-level Attribution field (source/referrer at last touch). This is the same gap flagged separately as a Meta Pixel attribution investigation, currently in progress with Graphedia: consent-gated pixel cookies likely mean purchases from visitors who decline marketing cookies fire into Meta's dataset but can't be linked back to the ad that drove them. Work is underway to confirm Conversions API (CAPI) status and recover attribution for these visits. Until resolved, treat Meta's self-reported purchase numbers as directional, not exact.
