Account Overview

The Kenny Fuels Meta Ads account has been running since late 2023 and has accumulated €19,938 in lifetime spend across 18 campaigns. The account is structured around four active sales campaigns — all using Advantage+ (Dynamic) delivery — covering firewood (B2C and B2B), compost, and sand & stones. A single pixel (Kenny Fuels Dataset) is live and firing across all product pages with full funnel tracking from ViewContent through to Purchase.

The headline numbers are exceptional: 135x ROAS year-to-date on €4,223 spend, generating €573,078 in attributed purchase value. Q1 (the heating season) delivered 230x ROAS at a €1.18 cost per purchase. However, the account is critically underfunded at €60/day across all four sales campaigns, has zero retargeting running, and has no paid coverage at all on gas and heating oil — Kenny Fuels' core product lines. The strategic priority entering June is to fix these structural gaps before the heating season returns in September.

Issues Found
What Needs Fixing

Structural problems identified during the baseline audit.

🚨

CRITICAL: Account is Severely Underfunded at €60/Day

Four campaigns × €15/day = €60/day total sales budget. At 135x ROAS, every €1 spent returns €135 in attributed revenue. The account is leaving significant money on the table every single day. Firewood B2C alone is returning 186x — there is no rational argument for running it at €15/day when the data is this clean.

🚨

CRITICAL: Zero Retargeting Running

BOF_Retargeting campaign is paused. YTD: 2,163 add-to-carts and 2,332 checkout initiations with no retargeting follow-up whatsoever. These are the highest-intent, cheapest-to-convert audiences available and they are being abandoned completely. Retargeting these warm audiences would generate conversions at a fraction of the cost of cold traffic.

🚨

CRITICAL: Gas and Heating Oil — Core Products — Have No Active Ads

Kenny Fuels' primary business is fuel — gas and heating oil. Every gas and heating oil campaign is currently paused. The business's core product lines have zero paid visibility on Meta. This is particularly urgent for planning: heating season begins in September and campaigns need to be built, tested, and optimised before demand peaks.

⚠️

HIGH: Frequency Rising to Dangerous Levels in Q2

Account frequency rose from 3.43 in Q1 to 6.35 in Q2, with last-90-day frequency at 6.99. The same audiences are seeing ads nearly 7 times on average. Reach in Q2 (112,638) is 26% lower than Q1 (152,295) despite higher spend. The account is burning through its existing audiences rather than reaching new people. Creative fatigue and audience exhaustion are accelerating.

⚠️

HIGH: Single-Adset Structure With No CBO

Every campaign runs a 1:1:2 structure (1 campaign → 1 ad set → 2 ads). Budget is set at ad set level, not campaign level. There is no Campaign Budget Optimisation, no audience testing across ad sets, and no mechanism for Meta to self-allocate between audiences. This is leaving efficiency on the table and limiting what the algorithm can learn.

⚠️

HIGH: No Conversion API (CAPI) Installed

Only browser-side pixel tracking is in use. Post-iOS14, browser pixel tracking typically misses 15–30% of conversions due to tracking prevention. Given average order values in the €200–300+ range, each missed conversion represents significant misattribution. CAPI installation via the website platform (WooCommerce/Shopify plugin or direct API) should be a week-one priority.

⚠️

MEDIUM: "Compost Trees" Creative Being Starved of Budget

The Compost Trees ad is delivering 107x ROAS vs 59x for the main Compost ad — nearly double — yet is receiving only €65 in spend vs €442. Meta's delivery algorithm is allocating the majority of budget to the lower-performing creative. This needs to be corrected manually by restructuring the Compost campaign to give both creatives equal budget access.

⚠️

MEDIUM: Compost Funnel Has 31% Checkout Drop-Off

293 add-to-carts → 215 initiate checkouts → 149 purchases. 31% of people who start checkout do not complete it, compared to 3% on Firewood. This points to a pricing, delivery cost, or UX issue on the compost product page — not a campaign issue. Needs investigation at the website level before scaling the campaign.

⚠️

MEDIUM: B2C Geography Limited to 5 Counties

Firewood B2C and both new product campaigns target only Carlow, Dublin, Waterford, Wexford, and Wicklow. Firewood B2B covers all Ireland and delivers strong results (149x ROAS). There is an untested opportunity to expand B2C coverage to additional Irish counties — particularly those adjacent to the existing 5-county area.

⚠️

MEDIUM: Partner Pixel Access Still Pending

Pixel (614735617253875) is accessible via API but not yet visible in our Events Manager. Ciaran was emailed on May 26 to assign the pixel to our partner account via Business Settings → Data Sources → Pixels → Assign Partners. Without direct Events Manager access, we cannot set up CAPI, run event quality diagnostics, or build custom audiences from pixel data directly. Awaiting Ciaran's response.

Takeover Plan
Recommended Strategy

Prioritised actions for June 1 handover and beyond.

Week 1 — Immediate

Budget & Retargeting

Increase Firewood B2C and B2B to €40–50/day each — highest proven ROAS in the account. Reactivate BOF retargeting campaign targeting Add-to-Cart and checkout abandoners from the last 30 days. These are the warmest audiences available and currently being abandoned entirely. Reallocate Compost budget toward the "Compost Trees" creative which is delivering 2x the ROAS of the main Compost ad.

Week 1 — Immediate

Pixel Partner Access & CAPI

Follow up with Ciaran to complete pixel partner access assignment. Once Events Manager access is confirmed, install Conversion API via the website platform (WooCommerce/Shopify plugin or server-side integration). CAPI is expected to recover 15–30% more attributed conversions — at current order values, this is a meaningful gap to close before scaling spend.

Weeks 2–4

Gas & Heating Oil Campaigns

Launch Heating Oil and Gas campaigns — the core product lines have zero paid presence on Meta. Begin with a Heating Oil awareness and conversion campaign targeting the 5-county B2C area, then expand to all-Ireland for B2B. These campaigns need to be live, tested, and optimised before heating season demand returns in September–October.

Weeks 2–4

Creative & Structure Improvements

Create new creative variants modelled on the "Digger Bath" format — study what makes it work (6.47% CTR) and replicate for other products. Restructure to Campaign Budget Optimisation (CBO) to allow Meta to self-allocate across audiences. Address the Compost checkout drop-off by investigating the product page experience — pricing, delivery costs, and UX are the likely culprits.

Weeks 2–4

Audience Expansion

Frequency at 6.99 in the last 90 days means the current B2C audiences are near saturation. Expand geographic targeting to counties adjacent to the current 5-county B2C area. Build lookalike audiences from the purchaser list to reach new cold audiences similar to existing customers. Introduce video view retargeting (MOF layer) for people who have watched 75%+ of video content.

Q3 Strategic — Aug/Sep

Heating Season Preparation

The account's peak is Q1 (Jan–Mar). Preparation needs to begin in August. Build a proper full funnel for the heating season: TOF cold campaigns → MOF video viewer and engager retargeting → BOF ATC and checkout abandoner retargeting. Build seasonal creative around urgency, pricing, and local delivery. Have the account fully structured and in learning phase before October demand arrives.
