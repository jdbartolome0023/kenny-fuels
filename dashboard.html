🔧

Shopping Campaign — Conversion Tracking Audit In Progress

The Shopping Campaign has now recorded zero leads over five days of active spend (€53.61 so far this week, €85.29 last week). This is not a tracking delay — it is a consistent pattern. We are auditing the conversion setup to determine whether the problem is with how leads are being recorded, the product feed quality, or an issue with the landing page that is preventing visitors from making an enquiry. A recommendation on whether to pause or restructure this campaign will follow once the audit is complete.

🔧

Gas Bottle & Cylinders — Bid Reduction Test Underway

This campaign spent €195 in the first five days of this week and has produced 5 leads at a cost of €39 each. Bids have been reviewed and a reduction of approximately 15–20% is being applied to bring the cost per lead down. This campaign was generating over 50 leads per week just three weeks ago at a much lower cost, so restoring that efficiency is a clear goal. We expect to see the effect of the bid change reflected in next week's numbers.

✓

Tested Search Campaign — Maintained and Monitored

This campaign continues to be the strongest performer in the account. Through five days this week it has generated 8 leads at €9.34 each — and that figure will improve once the full week's data is in. The "Kenny Fuels" ad group within this campaign is responsible for the vast majority of leads. The campaign settings are stable and we are actively looking at increasing its daily budget to generate more leads at the same efficient rate.

🔧

Zero-Conversion Ad Groups in Tested Search — Under Review

Within the Tested Search Campaign, several ad groups are spending budget without producing leads — including "Fuel Oil For Home Heating" (€12.91 this week, 0 leads), "Home Heating Oil Delivery" (€4.87, 0 leads), and others. These groups are generating clicks but not enquiries. We are reviewing the search terms they are matching to determine whether to pause them, tighten the targeting with negative keywords, or replace the ads. Redirecting this budget to the "Kenny Fuels" ad group — which converts reliably — would improve the overall campaign efficiency.
