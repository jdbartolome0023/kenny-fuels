Meta-Attributed Sales: Two Ways of Counting

Meta Ads reports what it thinks it drove. The site's own order data (WooCommerce) reports what it actually credits to a Meta/paid social source. The two numbers disagree by a wide margin, and that gap is not going to close.

Source Purchases Revenue % of Total Site Revenue
Meta Ads (self-reported) 28 €6,805 27.6%
WooCommerce's own attribution 5 €731 3.0%
Gap ~5.6x ~9.3x

Graphedia confirmed this week that visitors who decline marketing cookies cannot legally be tracked for ad attribution, by any method, under Irish ePrivacy/GDPR rules. This is a permanent limitation, not a bug in progress. A server-side Conversions API build is underway, but it will only improve tracking for visitors who've already consented; it will not close this gap for visitors who decline. The gap above also isn't purely "sales lost to consent": it includes traffic from completely unrelated non-Meta sources too, which WooCommerce's attribution can't separate out. Treat the WooCommerce-attributed figures as the more reliable performance measure going forward.

Attribution Gap Scope Narrowed With Graphedia

Followed up on the Meta attribution investigation. Graphedia confirmed that visitors declining marketing cookies cannot be legally tracked back to an ad by any method, including the server-side Conversions API fix we'd proposed, under Irish ePrivacy/GDPR rules. Agreed to proceed with a CAPI build that respects existing consent (improves tracking reliability for consented visitors) rather than push for a workaround. This is now understood as a permanent limitation to report around, not a bug to keep chasing.

✓

BOF Retargeting Frequency Resolved

Frequency dropped to 4.74 from 5.02 following the June 29 audience expansion to 90 days: back under the 5.0 healthy threshold. No further action needed this week.
